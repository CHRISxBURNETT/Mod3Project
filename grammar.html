Grammar

Gerunds A gerund is a verbal noun. A gerund is a word form that is derived from a verb but that functions as a noun, in English ending in -ing Verb + -ing Note: Do not confuse gerund with a continuous verb Ex: She was running in the park

    6 Types of Gerunds
  1. 1. Subject-can be the subject of the sentence like a noun
    2. a. Running is good for your heart.
  2. 2. Object-can be the object the action being done
    3. a. He hates waking up early.
  3. 3. Subject Complement-Giving more information to the subject
    4. a. What I hate most is repeating myself
  4. 4. Object Complement-Giving more information to the object
    5. a. I saw Jim riding his bike.
  5. 5. Object of a Preposition-After a preposition always use the gerund
    6. a. I'm interested in improving myself.
    b. I am excited about ______
  6. 6. Object of a Possessive-Showing ownership
    7. a. She doesn’t like your bossing her around. b. He doesn’t like your cooking.
